Rock • Paper • Scissors Minus One — How to play

This is a two-hand variant of rock–paper–scissors. You select two hands (Hand 1 and Hand 2), and the computer secretly selects two hands as well. After both sides have picked, you must choose which of your two hands to keep. The remaining hand from each side is compared to determine the round winner.

Steps:

  1. On the left, pick your Hand 1 and Hand 2 (each can be rock, paper, or scissors).
  2. Click Show hands. The computer’s two hands are revealed and your two hands are shown as buttons.
  3. Choose which of your two hands to keep by clicking the corresponding hand button (you have ~4.56 seconds).
  4. The computer then selects one of its two hands, using a simple predictive AI. The final hands are compared, and the result is displayed as Win, Loss, or Tie.
  5. Score updates (Wins / Losses / Ties). Use Reset Score to clear.

Controls & UI notes

  • Show hands — reveal both player's and computer's two hands and starts the 4.56s timer for you to pick.
  • Click one of your two revealed hand buttons to keep it. That triggers the round resolution.
  • Reset Score — clears local storage and resets displayed wins/losses/ties.
