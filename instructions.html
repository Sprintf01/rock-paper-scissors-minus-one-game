Rock • Paper • Scissors Minus One — How to play

Summary: This is a two-hand variant of rock–paper–scissors. You choose two hands (Hand 1 and Hand 2). The computer secretly chooses two hands as well. After both sides pick, you must remove one hand (shout “minus one”) — the remaining single hand from each side is compared to decide the round.

Step-by-step (quick)

  1. On the left, pick your Hand 1 and Hand 2 (each can be rock, paper, or scissors).
  2. Click Show hands. The computer’s two hands are revealed and your two hands are shown as buttons.
  3. Choose which of your two hands to keep by clicking the corresponding hand button (you have ~4.56 seconds).
  4. After you pick, the remaining computer hand is chosen (the site randomly picks one of the computer's two hands) and winner/tie is displayed.
  5. Score updates (Wins / Losses / Ties). Use Reset Score to clear.

Detailed rules

  • Rock beats Scissors; Paper beats Rock; Scissors beats Paper.
  • If both final hands are the same, the round is a tie.
  • If you fail to choose which hand to keep before the timer ends, you are disqualified for that round and the computer gains a win.
  • If you don't click both Hand 1 and Hand 2 before pressing Show hands, an alert will prompt you to choose both hands.

Examples

Example 1: You pick rock and paper. Computer reveals scissors and paper.
  • If you keep rock → computer's selected final hand may be scissors or paper; rock loses to paper, beats scissors — result depends.
  • If you keep paper → paper ties with paper or beats rock; in this example, if computer final is paper → tie, if scissors → you lose.

Controls & UI notes

  • Show hands — reveal both player's and computer's two hands and starts the 4.56s timer for you to pick.
  • Click one of your two revealed hand buttons to keep it. That triggers the round resolution.
  • Reset Score — clears local storage and resets displayed wins/losses/ties.
  • The computer chooses its final hand randomly between its two choices (50/50).

FAQ

Q: What happens if I click only one hand then Show hands?
A: The game alerts you to select both hands before proceeding.
Q: Where is the rule “minus one” enforced?
A: After Show hands, you must click which of your two hands you keep (effectively "minus one"). If you don't, the timer code penalizes you.
